Discover the Art of Surface Finishes

At Urblo, we believe stone isn’t just a material – it’s a canvas. In our new “Material Mastery” series, we’re diving deep into how surface treatments unlock endless design possibilities. First up: Bluestone, reimagined through 8 finishes.

One Stone, Eight Personalities

From rugged to refined, see how finishes transform bluestone’s color, texture, and tone:

Sawn – Sleek, industrial edge.

Honed – Smooth, muted elegance.

Rock Face – Raw, natural contours.

Rippling-Fine – Subtle wave-like grooves.

Rippling-Rough – Bold, tactile ripples.

Sandblasted – Matte, slip-resistant grit.

Bush Hammer-Rough – Dramatic, pitted texture.

Bush Hammer-Fine – Uniform, low-profile grain.
 

Case Study | West Side Place

This landmark project blended bluestone and granite across:

  • Sawn & Honed planters for contrast.

  • Bush Hammer-Rough accents to echo the city’s grit.

  • Polished seating for a modern touch.

Result? A cohesive yet dynamic streetscape proving that one stone can wear many hats.
 

Project: West Side Place, Melbourne VIC
Designer & Consultant: Rush Wright Associates
Contractor: Delta Group
Completion: 2023
 

Why It Matters

  • Design Flexibility: Match finishes to function (e.g., slip-resistant for walkways, polished for focal points).

  • Cost Efficiency: Achieve visual variety without multiple materials.

  • Sustainability: Maximize one stone’s potential, reduce waste
